Key requirements

  • Check-in (required, no VHF check-in): from 1100 Thu — text 516-945-9209 or the Regatta Desk WhatsApp with boat name, sail number, division. Message once only. Not starting → send "DNS".
  • YB tracker: mount the OA-supplied transponder on deck with clear sky view (pushpit/davit). Report any failure to the RC. Return to Sea Cliff YC after finishing — by Fri Jul 31 per the SI (NOR says Aug 1) — or the replacement cost is charged.
  • AIS: required by SER, must transmit Zammermoos (entered name), not an MMSI or former name. If it can't, text/WhatsApp the transmitted name, entered name and sail number before the start. [NP] A station failing to receive tracker/AIS signals is not grounds for protest or redress.
  • Night rules (NOR Amd 1): between sunset and sunrise, RRS Part 2 is replaced by IRPCAS/COLREGS.
  • Course (ALI, 207 nm scoring): only mandatory mark is Ambrose Channel Red Bell Q R "14" (40°31.68′N / 74°00.57′W) to port; all other government marks may be disregarded.
  • Montauk report (required): as soon as possible after Montauk Point Light bears 270° magnetic, phone/text/WhatsApp: name, sail number, division, rounding time.
  • Finish: blue flag on the finish vessel ~0.075 nm due W of the Glen Cove Breakwater light (40°51.72′N / 73°39.62′W) to the breakwater. The breakwater extends ~25 ft west of the green flashing light — leave room. Hail the finish vessel on VHF 69 when approaching; at night also flash a light on the sail numbers.
  • Crew: minimum 4 aboard (our division). Sail numbers on all sails per RRS App G (tape OK).
  • Equipment: US Sailing SER for Coastal races applies (working AIS transponder included); self-righting, properly rigged and ballasted.
  • Penalties: RRS 44.3 scoring penalty applies — for Part 2 incidents, OCS, or tracking/AIS breaches the PC may add 2% to elapsed time instead of DSQ. Protests: delivered in person to the Regatta Desk at Sea Cliff YC within 2 h of our finish; hearings from 1100 Sun.
  • Overdue: not finished by 1200 Sun Jul 27 → call/text/WhatsApp boat name, division and position (avoids a SAR callout).
  • Withdrawal: notify the Regatta Desk immediately by phone/text/WhatsApp.

Race setup — start times & distances

Starts default to scratch-sheet warning signal + 5 min. If a group is postponed, edit its start here — every margin updates. Distances are scoring distances from the SIs (ALI 207 nm; ATI 230 nm per the scoring section). Only values you change are kept on this device; everything else follows the shipped scratch sheet.

They owe us — they can finish ahead of us by anything less than the margin (clock time) and we still beat them We owe them — we must finish more than the margin ahead of them (clock time)

How margins are figured: margin = (our start − their start) + (our rating × our distance − their rating × their distance). Because start staggers are folded in, every margin reads directly in finish-line clock time: green means they can cross ahead of us by anything less than that margin and we still win on corrected; red means we must cross more than that margin ahead of them. Finishing separated by exactly the margin is a corrected-time dead heat.

ORC Division 0 is scored under ORC APH, a different handicap system — corrected times there are not comparable to PHRF, so no margin is shown. Nomad (Div 6) sails the Around-the-Islands course; her margin uses the 230 nm ATI distance and is a paper comparison across different courses.
