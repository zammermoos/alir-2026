They owe us — they can finish ahead of us by anything less than the margin (clock time) and we still beat them We owe them — we must finish more than the margin ahead of them (clock time)

How margins are figured: margin = (our start − their start) + (our rating × our distance − their rating × their distance). Because start staggers are folded in, every margin reads directly in finish-line clock time: green means they can cross ahead of us by anything less than that margin and we still win on corrected; red means we must cross more than that margin ahead of them. Finishing separated by exactly the margin is a corrected-time dead heat.

ORC Division 0 is scored under ORC APH, a different handicap system — corrected times there are not comparable to PHRF, so no margin is shown. Nomad (Div 6) sails the Around-the-Islands course; her margin uses the 230 nm ATI distance and is a paper comparison across different courses.
